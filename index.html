I am a Ph.D. candidate at Johns Hopkins University where I work with the CLASS experiment. At CLASS, we aim to map out the minuscule polarization fluctuations emanating from the distant universe (the cosmic microwave background). I'm actively engaged in the data pipeline development and analysis.

Before that, I received my Bachelor's Degree in Physics from Peking University in 2017. As an undergrad, I was constantly wondering about dwarf galaxies, Galactic X-ray emissions, and neutron stars.
